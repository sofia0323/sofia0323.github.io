Introduction

The Netherlands, also known as Holland, is a country located in Western Europe. It is bordered by Germany to the east, Belgium to the south, and the North Sea to the northwest. The Netherlands is known for its flat landscape, canals, tulip fields, windmills, and cycling routes.

Environment

Current Issues

  • Water pollution from industrial and agricultural sources
  • Air pollution from vehicles and refining activities
  • Greenhouse gas emissions
  • Acid rain
  • Loss of biodiversity

International Agreements

  • Party to: Air Pollution, Biodiversity, Climate Change, Climate Change-Kyoto Protocol, Desertification, Endangered Species, Hazardous Wastes, Law of the Sea, Marine Dumping, Marine Life Conservation, Ozone Layer Protection, Wetlands, Whaling

Government

The Netherlands is a parliamentary constitutional monarchy, which means the country is headed by a monarch (King or Queen) and a parliamentary system. The current monarch is King Willem-Alexander, and the head of government is the Prime Minister.

Economy

The Netherlands has a highly developed, diverse, and market-oriented economy. The country is a major exporter of agricultural products, manufactured goods, and natural resources.
